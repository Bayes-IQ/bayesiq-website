# BayesIQ Brand Voice

## Tone

- **Technical but accessible** — write for a senior data engineer or analytics lead, not a boardroom. Assume the reader knows what ETL means but explain your specific approach.
- **Confident without hype** — state what we do and what it achieves. Skip superlatives. Let the specificity of our results speak for itself.
- **Specific over vague** — numbers, examples, named technologies, concrete deliverables. "We found 7 broken metrics in 2 weeks" beats "we improved data quality."
- **Slightly opinionated** — we have a point of view on how data quality should work. Data systems should be tested and validated like code. This isn't controversial — it's just rarely done.

## Language Rules

- Say "audit" not "solution"
- Say "we found 7 broken metrics" not "we improved data quality"
- Say "telemetry validation" not "data governance"
- Say "fix plan" not "strategic roadmap"
- Say "broken" or "wrong" not "suboptimal"
- Never use: revolutionary, game-changing, cutting-edge, leverage, synergy, unlock, empower
- Avoid: "AI-powered" as a lead adjective — we use AI, but it's the method, not the brand identity
- Avoid: passive voice when describing what we do — "we audit" not "audits are performed"

## Visual Identity

- Clean, minimal, typographic — think Stripe/Linear, not enterprise SaaS
- Monochrome palette with one accent color
- Data visualizations and diagrams should clarify, not decorate
- No stock photos of people looking at screens
- No gradient blob backgrounds
- Generous whitespace — let the content breathe

## Reference Sites

For design inspiration, not copying:
- stripe.com — clarity, typography, developer trust
- linear.app — clean product marketing, technical audience
- vercel.com — minimalism, speed, developer-first
- posthog.com — technical product, approachable tone
