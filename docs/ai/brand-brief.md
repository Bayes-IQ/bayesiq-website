# BayesIQ Brand Brief — Visual Design Reference

## Identity
BayesIQ is a governed analytics consultancy and platform. We find broken
metrics and broken pipelines, then deliver the fix path.

## Personality
Trustworthy, technical, precise, calm. Not flashy, not startup-y, not
enterprise-gray. The visual analogy is a well-organized control room —
everything in its place, nothing wasted, confidence in the system.

## Audience
Senior analytics leaders (VP Analytics, CDO, Head of Data) at mid-market
companies who've been burned by bad metrics. They're skeptical of dashboards
and allergic to buzzwords.

## Design Principles
1. **Density over decoration** — information-rich, not visually noisy
2. **Evidence over assertion** — show the governance chain, don't just claim quality
3. **Calm confidence** — cool grays, deliberate blue accents, generous whitespace
4. **Progressive disclosure** — don't overwhelm; let users dig deeper when ready

## Color Language
- **Primary palette:** Cool grays (bayesiq-50 through bayesiq-950)
- **Accent:** Blue (#3b82f6) — used sparingly for CTAs and focus states
- **Status:** Standard semantic colors (green=success, amber=warning, red=error)
- **Dark sections:** bayesiq-900/950 backgrounds with light text — Header, Footer, CTA blocks, platform page

## Typography
- **Body:** Inter (sans-serif) — clean, technical, highly legible
- **Display/Headings:** DM Sans — slightly warmer, distinguishes headings from body
- **Code:** JetBrains Mono — used for technical identifiers (artifact names, finding IDs)

## Anti-Pattern Watchlist
These patterns signal generic SaaS design rather than BayesIQ's identity:
1. 3-column icon+text feature grid (unless content is genuinely parallel)
2. Testimonial carousels
3. "Trusted by" logo strips (unless real client logos exist)
4. Gradient backgrounds without brand justification
5. Stock-photo hero imagery
6. "Sign up NOW" urgency language
7. Decorative animations that don't communicate information

## Red Flags for AI Review
- Anything that feels like a template rather than purposeful design
- Gratuitous shadows, glows, or border effects
- Text that sounds like marketing copy instead of expert communication
- Cluttered sections where the eye has no resting point
- CTAs that feel desperate rather than confident
- Inconsistent spacing between similar elements
- Dark/light section boundaries that feel abrupt rather than intentional
